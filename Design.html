1. ANADIGILOX

Introduction

Grab your breadboards, sharpen your wires and assemble your ICs to enter the turf of digital electronics. Open the gates of logic and put on your thinking shoes for ANADIGILOGIX will make you go running over to the basics of circuit designing. It consists of a practical life problem statement based on which you have to design a circuit fulfilling all requirements of the problem.

Problem statement will be displayed on the website 2-3 days before the event.

Rules

  1. There should be at most 3 members in a team.
  2. The circuit has to be designed using digital devices.
  3. Microcontrollers and microprocessors are not allowed.
  4. The circuit can be made on PROTEUS(software) , breadboard or soldered as per participants wish.
  5. Participants are required to present a detailed description of their circuit.
  6. Coordinators reserve the right to disqualify any team indulging in misbehaviour or violating any rules.
  7. The decision of the judging committee will be final and bounding.
  8. Participants must bring the D.C. power source with them. The organisers will provide 220V A.C. supply. No adapters will be provided; the participants must bring their own.

JUDGING CRITERIA

  • Circuit Design
  • Cost
  • Presentation
  • Reliability
  • Efficiency

CERTIFICATE POLICY

  • Certificate of Excellence will be awarded to the winners.
  • Certificates of Participation will be given to all the teams.
  • The teams which get disqualified for disobeying any of the competition rules will not be considered for any certificate.

2. Bridge it up

Introduction

Modelling is an art of epitomizing a complex process ,who interprets many abstract and concrete ideas . One of the most sought after event in technical fest history is back again with bang. Model Bridges are intended to be a simplified version of real world . Design the most efficient bridge to accept a load ,any position and to permit that load to travel the bridge. Give yourself and us a legit reason to brag your bridge to be the best.
